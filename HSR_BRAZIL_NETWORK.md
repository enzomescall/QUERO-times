# Rede Ferroviaria de Alta Velocidade do Brasil (Brazil High-Speed Rail Network)

This document outlines a phased, cost-conscious national high-speed rail network for Brazil, designed for a maximum operating speed of 250-280 km/h using standard gauge (1435 mm). Routes follow existing transport corridors (BR-101, BR-116, BR-381, BR-040, BR-050, BR-153, and others) to minimise land acquisition and environmental licensing costs. Infrastructure is designed for incremental upgrades: Tier 1 (Southeast core) is built first, generating revenue and operational expertise before Tier 2 (expansion) and subsequent tiers proceed. Stations are co-located with existing airports, bus terminals, or urban rail systems wherever possible.

## Tier 1 -- Core Southeast

### LEX-01 -- Linha Expressa Litoral

**Corridor:** Rio de Janeiro -- Sao Paulo -- Campinas. Follows BR-101 (Rio-Santos) and BR-116 (Via Dutra) corridors with a northern arc into Campinas.

**Terminals:**

**Stations:**

- Nova Iguaçu (via connecting shuttle from Central) -- Minor -- Not on main line; served by branch
- Sao Jose dos Campos (Centro, near Rodoviaria Velha) -- Major
- Aparecida/Guaratingueta (Estacao Aparecida, adjacent to BR-116) -- Minor
- Sao Paulo (Terminal Rodoviario Tietê / Metro Armênia area) -- Major -- Shared with Metro Linha 1 Azul, EMTU bus terminal
- Jundiai (Estacao Jundiai, near Rodoviaria) -- Minor
- Campinas (Terminal Ramos de Azevedo, adjoining Rodoviaria) -- Major

**Express Services:**
- Expresso Litoral: Rio de Janeiro -- Sao Paulo -- Campinas (stops only at SJC, SP, Campinas)
- Expresso Cone Sul: Rio de Janeiro -- Sao Paulo non-stop (limited peak services)
- Semi-Expresso Vale: Rio -- Aparecida -- SJC -- SP (stops at intermediate Vale do Paraiba cities)

**Connections:**
- LEX-02 (Linha Expressa Central) at Rio de Janeiro (Central)
- LEX-04 (Linha Expressa Planalto) at Campinas
- LEX-03 (Linha Expressa Sul) at Sao Paulo

**Estimated Cost (USD):** $ 15.2 billion (Terrain: Mixed -- rolling Paraiba valley, mountain escarpment at Serra do Mar, urban approaches in Rio and SP)

**Notes:**
The most heavily travelled corridor in South America. The section between Rio and Sao Paulo carries roughly 20 million passengers per year by bus and air. The 280 km/h design allows Rio-Sao Paulo in 1 h 40 min and Rio-Campinas in 2 h 10 min. Urban sections in Rio (25 km) and Sao Paulo (30 km) are elevated on concrete viaducts alongside existing rail corridors to avoid tunnelling. The crossing of the Serra do Mar between Rio and the Paraiba valley requires a 12 km tunnel at grade -- the single most expensive engineered section on the line. Phase 1 opens Rio-Sao Paulo; Phase 2 extends to Campinas.

Operating speed is 280 km/h on the plateau sections, reduced to 220 km/h through the escarpment tunnels. Sao Paulo-Curitiba in approximately 1 h 50 min.

### Key Design Decisions

- **Standard gauge (1435 mm)** chosen for rolling stock interoperability across the entire network. All lines use the same gauge, allowing trainsets to operate on any electrified corridor.
- **Electrification:** 25 kV 60 Hz AC overhead catenary (standard for all lines).
- **Maximum gradient:** 3.5% (except LEX-02 and LEX-06 mountain sections where 4.0% is permitted with reduced speed).
- **Minimum curve radius:** 4,000 m (280 km/h), 2,500 m (250 km/h), 1,500 m (220 km/h mountain sections).
- **Tunnelling minimised** by following existing road corridors. Only three significant tunnels are required: Serra do Mar (LEX-01, 12 km), Serra do Paranapiacaba (LEX-03, 6 km), and Serra do Mar south of Curitiba (LEX-06, 4 km).
- **Shared stations** at Rio Central (SuperVia + Metro), Sao Paulo Tietê (Metro), Campinas Ramos de Azevedo (Bus terminal), Brasilia Rodoferroviaria (Metro DF), Curitiba Rodoferroviaria, Recife Central (MetroRec), and Fortaleza Jose de Alencar (Metro Fortaleza), among others.
